OSLC Product Lifecycle Management Version 1.0. Part 3: Constraints defines the standard OSLC constratint on the RDF OSLC Product Lifecycle Management Version 1.0 vocabulary terms.

Introduction

This section specifies the constraints for the PLM resources. The resource properties are not limited to the ones defined in this specification PLM Servers may provide additional properties. It is recommended that any additional properties exist in their own unique namespace and not use the namespaces defined in these specifications.

RDF vocabularies define the terms and resources for a domain of interest, life-cycle management in the case of OSLC PLM. These vocabularies are often specified in an open manner, without providing information such as property domain and range assertions, cardinalities, etc. This helps keep the vocabulary applicable for a wide range of uses and furthering integration with other vocabularies.

However, it is often desirable to closed down a vocabulary with specific constraints to facilitate using the vocabulary for a specific purpose. This document specifies the constraints for using the OSLC PLM vocabulary in OSLC. Different sets of constraints may be applied to a vocabulary in order to tailor its use, without overly constraining the vocabulary for other usages.

These constraints apply to the core vocabulary defined in OSLC Product Lifecycle Management Version 1.0. Part 2: Vocabulary.

EffectivityValueType enumeration

The oslc_plm:effectiveValueType property on an oslc_plm:Effectivity record names the kind of range the record carries. oslc_plm:EffectivityValueType is an OSLC enumeration defined in plm-vocab.ttl: the class itself is open in the vocabulary, and the Effectivity shape closes it via oslc:range oslc_plm:EffectivityValueType together with an oslc:allowedValue list of the five enumeration members below.

The enumeration members:

  • oslc_plm:Date — range values are xsd:date.
  • oslc_plm:UnitNumber — range values are xsd:integer unit numbers, interpreted within the namespace of effectiveForEndItem.
  • oslc_plm:SerialNumber — range values are xsd:string serial numbers, interpreted within the namespace of effectiveForEndItem.
  • oslc_plm:Lot — range values are xsd:string lot identifiers.
  • oslc_plm:Model — range values are xsd:string model identifiers.

Each member is an individual of type oslc_plm:EffectivityValueType (per the OSLC enumeration pattern, not its own rdfs:Class); the URI is the identifier and rdfs:label carries the display name. Members have no properties of their own and therefore no oslc:ResourceShape.

Constraints on Other OSLC Domain Resources

oslc_am:Resource

The OSLC PLM specification contributes a new common link type, oslc_am:realizes, as a property on oslc_am:Resource. The link mirrors the UML and SysML notion of Realization: the source — an architecture-modelling resource — is the realizer, and the target is the realized resource. The canonical cross-domain use is a SysML PartDefinition (exposed as an oslc_am:Resource via the OASIS OSLC SysML v2 vocabulary) realizing an oslc_plm:Part: the architecture-model element represents or fulfils the PLM master record.

The property is defined on oslc_am:Resource only. PLM resources (oslc_plm:Part, oslc_plm:PartUsage, oslc_plm:LogicalDesign, oslc_plm:PhysicalDesign) are targets of this link, not bearers — they appear as the object of an oslc_am:realizes assertion made by an AM resource.

Typical uses include:

  • A SysML PartDefinition realizing an oslc_plm:Part;
  • An architecture model element realizing a PLM oslc_plm:PartUsage that participates in a specific BOM line;
  • A physical-architecture oslc_am:Resource realizing a logical-architecture oslc_am:Resource (the ARCADIA Realization Link pattern, AM-internal).

OSLC AM implementations that wish to support the PLM domain SHOULD recognise the oslc_am:realizes property on oslc_am:Resource and its subtypes.

Note on namespacing. The other common link types referenced on PLM shapes (derives, elaborates, refine, external, satisfy, trace) live in the legacy jazz_am: namespace (http://jazz.net/ns/dm/linktypes#). realizes is being introduced here in the OASIS-standard oslc_am: namespace (http://open-services.net/ns/am#). The OASIS OSLC-OP TC may at some point choose to migrate the legacy link types to oslc_am: for consistency; that is a separate decision and is not pre-empted by this contribution.
